THE LANDSCAPE

WHY AI MATTERS TO MARKETS

AI is the largest capital expenditure cycle in history.

Amazon, Alphabet, Microsoft, Meta, and Oracle have guided to $685B in combined 2026 capex — and cumulative AI data center investment is on track to exceed $4 trillion by 2030.

It touches every layer of the economy.

From the raw silicon in the ground, to the chips in a data center, to the AI agent booking your flight — this is a full-stack economic transformation.

The market is repricing everything.

Semiconductor stocks (SOXX) up ~13% YTD. Software stocks (IGV) down ~20% YTD. A 33-point spread — the same AI wave is creating winners and losers simultaneously.

Sources: Company earnings guidance (Q4 2025/Q1 2026), Bloomberg, S&P Global Market Intelligence.

IMAGE: chatbot vs. agent comparison illustration
Chatbots (2023–2024)
  • Single query, human-in-the-loop

  • ~1x compute per user
  • Limited tool integration
Agents (2025+)
  • Multi-step autonomous workflows

Implication: Inference compute demand explodes. Software vendors must adapt or be replaced.

THE RISKS

SUPPLY CHAIN FRAGILITY

Three critical risk vectors threatening the AI buildout.
Geographic Concentration
  • Taiwan (TSMC): ~90% advanced chips
  • South Korea: ~70% DRAM, ~62% HBM
  • Netherlands (ASML): sole EUV supplier
  • Single points of failure across the stack
US Reshoring
  • CHIPS Act: $52.7B authorized
  • ~$33.7B committed so far
  • TSMC Arizona: delayed, over budget
  • Still years from meaningful capacity
Energy / Hormuz Crisis
  • Feb 28–Mar 16 escalation timeline
  • Oil to $126/bbl
  • Qatar helium supply disrupted
  • S. Korea: 64.7% helium from Qatar
Hormuz Scenarios: 2-week resolution (minimal) | 2–8 weeks (spot +40–60%) | Equipment damage at Ras Laffan (1+ year structural shortage)
Sources: SIA, CHIPS Act tracker, Reuters, USGS.

KEY TAKEAWAYS

01
The capex cycle is real and financing itself.

$685B in 2026. These companies have the cash flows to sustain it.

02
Follow the atoms, not the bits (for now).

Semiconductors and memory are the immediate winners. Software's reckoning is underway.

03
Bottlenecks shift.

Today it's power and HBM. Tomorrow it could be helium, packaging, or regulatory gridlock.

04
Get intentional about exposure.

AI is not one trade — it's an entire supply chain. Pick your layer.
